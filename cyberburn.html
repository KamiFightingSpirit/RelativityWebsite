Company - Cyberburn:

Title - Founder and CEO
Years - 2009 through 2014

Starting out in my parents' garage and with just $400 (which at the time was about 1/5th of my net worth), over the next few years I built my company selling rave and party supplies into one that had five employees, had over $430,000 in annual sales, and an incredible 37% EBT.

I differentiated my company by making my own unique items in house (LED Gas Masks for example). This drove traffic to my storefront and enabled me to setup relationships with my competitors via dropshipping, where they forwarded sales receipts to me and I made and shipped items directly to the customer. Over time these relationships grew and I began dropshipping ready made items that my competitors did not wish to store in their inventory.

Stress levels were unreal but the lessons learned were invaluable. It taught me an ownership mentality and forced me to plumb the depths of self-motivation; an experience that made me confident I could teach myself programming. Running my own company was also the first real area where I dabbled in webwork, using HTML and CSS to design my product pages.
