History:

My first foray into coding really began on Neopets. For those unfamiliar, Neopets is a website in a similar vein as Tamagotchi pets, where you choose and care for a virtual pet. There, I set up the HTML links for a mall that I ran by mapping them to marketing images I created. The "malls" on the site were player creations that joined eight or so shops together; each receiving the benefit of traffic from the others. Being the entrepreneurial spirit that I am, I went one step further and positioned myself as head of a single mall consisting of multiple smaller malls, whereby I could have around 30 stores linking to my shop and generating about 500k Neopoints per day. Greedy much?

While running my own company Cyberburn, I leveraged and extended this knowledge. There, I wrote the product pages and dabbled in the SEO for my websites. While this was only dipping my toe into the programming world, it did pique my interest and it became something that I always wanted to study but never seemed to have the time to.

Upon entering my first corporate position at BlackRock, I used SQL daily. I quickly discovered that this was the most enjoyable part of the job and spent most of my time studying SQL and writing queries. This interest continued at Bridgewater where I not only continued to use SQL, albeit sparingly, but also built bespoke analytical models. The models within Finance use the same analytical frameworks as putting together good, clean code. This has assisted me significantly as I study and improve my programming.

After leaving Bridgewater, I decided to give programming my all. From here I have gone through FreeCodeCamp.org's courses as well as Colt Steele's Web Developer Bootcamp and The Modern React Bootcamp on Udemy. I continue to study daily and am currently targeting data structure and algorithm knowledge. I also volunteer for the League of Amazing Programmers. Which is a wonderful non-profit that teaches kids how to code in Java and gives them the opportunity to study to be a software engineer while still in highschool! If you wish to learn more about this wonderful orgranization click HERE. I absolutely love learning programming and already have far too many ideas that I wish to implement, this site being the first.
